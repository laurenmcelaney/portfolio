The Designer

I was 13 when I got my first laptop. It was the newly launched apple white macbook. Before receiving this gift, I frequently used my mother’s imac and developed a fascination for apple computers. Getting my very own laptop wasn’t just a new toy to me, it was the start of my dive into web design and coding. My life outside my bedroom in which I spent hours writing html and css wasn’t easy. Discovering that there was another world online, one that brought together similar interests through forums, was the thing I needed to help me find my passion. I taught myself the ins and outs of adobe photoshop, php code, and the process that one must take to build a website from the ground up. My creative interests grew and web design fostered an outlet in which I was able to develop designs and projects that I was proud of.

Design gave me a voice when I didn’t have one. It fuels my desire to create future projects and give voice to those who were like my 13-year-old self. Web development and design is constantly changing, but one thing that will never stop is my passion to form a world where words don’t need to be spoken but rather are seen through the visuals we create.
